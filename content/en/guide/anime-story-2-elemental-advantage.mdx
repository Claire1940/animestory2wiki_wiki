<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Primary Cycle: Fire, Nature, and Water Meta">

The core of your team-building will revolve around the Fire-Nature-Water triangle. As of July 2026, the power creep has introduced units that not only exploit these advantages but also provide massive team-wide buffs.

**Fire Element Dynamics**
Fire units are currently dominated by **Leona** and **Devil Cyborg (Meltdown)**. While Leona provides consistent burn damage, Devil Cyborg is a high-risk tactical unit. His ability to sacrifice HP for a 400% damage burst makes him a staple for clearing waves in record time.

**Nature Element Dominance**
With the introduction of **Golden Wind (Giorno)**, the Nature element has surged in popularity. Giorno’s "Return to Zero" passive allows for a complete skip of the enemy's turn after three Life Seed blooms, which is arguably the strongest crowd-control mechanic in the game.

**Water Element Utility**
**Slime (Demon)** remains the undisputed king of Water. His Black Flames provide a 66% damage boost and a massive 50% buff to all other Water units on the field. If you are running a Water-heavy team for Fire-based Raids, Slime is non-negotiable.

| **Nature** | Golden Wind | Return to Zero / Revive | High-Level Tower, Bosses |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Tip">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Specialized Elements: Spark, Light, and Dark">

Beyond the basic cycle, **anime story 2 elemental advantage** includes specialized elements that offer unique tactical benefits. These elements are often found on the most elusive units in the game.

**The Spark Element**
Spark is unique because it specifically counters Water without being part of the Nature cycle. **Sound Breather Master** is the premier Spark unit, though he is known for his RNG-heavy "Musical Score" passive which can either double his damage or halve it. For more consistent Spark performance, **Tengen** is the preferred choice for Tower bosses.

**The Dark and Light Dichotomy**
Dark and Light units are the "glass cannons" of elemental matchups. They deal bonus damage to each other, meaning a Dark unit like **Eisen (Ascended)** will shred a Light boss but will also take massive damage in return.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Team Compositions and Synergy">

Building a team solely based on **anime story 2 elemental advantage** isn't enough; you must also consider unit synergies. A well-constructed team utilizes elemental buffs to multiply the damage of a single carry unit.

**The Burn/Flame Synergy**
Combining **Flame Lion** (Fire) with **Devil Cyborg** (Fire) creates a feedback loop where Flame Lion applies Burn and Cyborg provides a 50% damage buff to all Fire units. This is the gold standard for clearing Nature-heavy content.

**The CC Lock Strategy**
For difficult Tower floors, mixing elements can be beneficial if the passives align. Using **Kaizen (Ascended)** for a Dark-based stun alongside **Jolios (King)** for a freeze effect can keep enemies incapacitated while your elemental carries deal the heavy damage.
